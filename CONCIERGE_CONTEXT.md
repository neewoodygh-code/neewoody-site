# Carpentry Concierge — Project Context for Claude Code
*Session digest, 14 July 2026. Companion to CONCIERGE_SPEC.md — the spec says WHAT to build; this document says WHY, and where things stand. Read both before working on anything Concierge-related.*

## The vision (what Nuer is actually trying to achieve)

Nuer runs Neewoody Custom Woodwork (neewoodygh.com), a bespoke furniture business in Accra. His TikTok (@neewoodygh, ~12.1K followers) has organically become a channel for **Ghanaian carpenters**, not clients — comment sections full of tradespeople venting about shared pain points: clients disputing fair prices, scope disputes, wasted estimate time, comparing handmade work to China Mall imports.

**Carpentry Concierge** is the answer: a paid membership platform for Ghanaian carpenters. **The purpose of member intake is to build a community — a place where carpenters in Ghana collaborate, learn from each other, and get access to the tools built so far (directory, cutlist, estimator) and every tool built after.** The directory is the first expression of that community, not the whole point. Long-term components, in intended build order:

1. **Members directory** — carpenters find and hire each other (upholsterer in Tema needs a spray finisher for a week, etc.). Carpenters were already improvising this by posting phone numbers in TikTok comments — the demand is observed, not hypothetical. ✅ BUILT (Phase 1). **The directory is a two-sided labour market** (owner clarification, 2026-07-14): one side is people looking for work and apprenticeships; the other is startups/small businesses looking for workers. "Relatively qualified people near you" is the core promise — Phase 1's area + specialty filters on owner-vetted members already deliver the base of it. **Planned lightweight evolution (do NOT build until founder demand confirms the exact states):** an availability flag on profiles — e.g. *open to work / hiring / seeking apprenticeship / taking apprentices* — one column + filter chips. Let the founders' WhatsApp group reveal which states matter before adding it.
2. **Lone-worker safety check-in** (Phase 2) — carpenter logs a call-out before going to a consultation (client name, phone, location, expected duration), GPS check-in on arrival, check-out deadline; if overdue, system alerts their emergency contact via SMS + alerts Nuer. Built on the same pattern as the existing Neewoody Dispatch app (GPS depart/arrive/return/close). **The call-out record is the product, not just the alert:** if anything ever happens to a carpenter on a consultation, the record (who they went to see, where, when, expected return) is the starting point for an investigation. Design consequence for Phase 2: call-out records are retained after close (never auto-purged), timestamped, and admin-accessible. This is the differentiator — nobody offers this to informal-sector tradespeople in Ghana. **Do not build until ~15+ paid members exist.**
3. Later: structured knowledge sharing, eventually a carpentry academy. (Collaboration and peer learning happen in the WhatsApp community from day one — see below; the academy formalizes it later.) The suggestion portal idea from comments = a WhatsApp poll for now, NOT software.

**Tools tiering (decided 2026-07-14, after owner floated gating the wardrobe cutlist):**
- **The wardrobe cutlist calculator stays free forever.** It is the acquisition funnel (TikTok → cutlist → Concierge); gating it would put a wall at the exact moment of peak demonstrated value. This decision is settled — do not re-litigate it.
- **The member benefit on the free tool is persistence:** saved cutlists (save/reload named configurations) are member-only. ✅ BUILT 2026-07-14 (`saved_cutlists` migration + `/api/cutlists` CRUD + save/load UI on cutlist.html). "No recall or memory except for members" is now literally true.
- **New tools launch member-first or member-only:** Kitchen cutlist, TV-unit cutlist, and other aids to come have no funnel history — they ARE the membership's growing tool library. The free wardrobe tool is the taste; the library is the subscription.
- **Owner intent (2026-07-14): member versions of the dispatch and pricing tools are on the roadmap.** Critical constraint — share the *capability*, never the *instances*: Neewoody's dispatch (single-tenant KV, owner's crew/jobs/leads, Standing Instruction 5) and pricing tool (embeds the owner's private overhead/tier/rate economics) are NOT to be opened to members. Member versions are new multi-tenant builds on concierge-api/D1: (a) dispatch-for-members grows out of Phase 2's safety check-in (same GPS lifecycle pattern); (b) pricing-for-members = a "price your job properly" calculator where each member configures their OWN overhead, crew rates, and margins — directly answers the #1 TikTok pain point (clients disputing fair prices). Neither is scheduled; both wait behind launch and the Phase 2 trigger.
- **The member landing page (directory) must give members things to DO** (owner requirement, 2026-07-14): direct access to the cutlist generator + their saved cutlists, a visible list of current benefits, and the upcoming-tools roadmap so members see what they're paying toward. ✅ BUILT (tools/benefits strip on directory.html).
- **Site prominence (owner-directed, 2026-07-14): the nav label "Tools" is now "Concierge" site-wide**, pointing at `tools.html` — reworked into the public **Concierge hub**: free tools (kept, unchanged) + membership section (benefits grid, founding offer GHS 50/mo first-50 locked vs GHS 100 after, `wa.me/…?text=FOUNDER` join CTA, member login link) + guest/member session bar. Homepage carries a restrained "For Carpenters" section → hub; every footer links to the hub. Efficiency principle: session-aware pages are ONLY the hub, cutlist, and `/concierge/*` — login.html's existing redirect handles routing logged-in members, so marketing pages stay static and light.
- **Guest/member bar rule (owner-ratified, 2026-07-14): every member tool page ships with the dual session bar.** No session → slim guest bar: one-line member pitch + "Member login →" linking to `/concierge/login.html?next=<this page's path>` (login honours same-site `?next=` returns, open-redirect guarded). Live session → dark member bar: member badge, the page's member actions (e.g. 📂 My Cutlists), "Directory →" link, and a **Log out** button. Rationale: tool pages are how people arrive (TikTok → tool), so each one must be a login entry point AND a properly closable session — never leave a page where a member can't log out or a guest can't log in. Reference implementation: cutlist.html (commit `2483686`). Apply to every future tool (kitchen cutlist, TV-unit cutlist, pricing calculator, …).

**The community layer is WhatsApp, deliberately.** Do not build chat, forums, or messaging — Ghanaian tradespeople live on WhatsApp and a custom platform would be a ghost town. The website is the directory + tools; WhatsApp is where members talk.

> **⚠ AMENDED by owner, 2026-07-15 (evening):** there will be **NO members' WhatsApp group** — owner: groups devolve into irrelevant media and greetings; "the website keeps everything direct — you need help with something, you post a job or check the tutorials page. This is going to be the Uber for carpenters." What survives from the original rule: **1:1 WhatsApp deep links remain the contact channel** (directory hiring, job applications, member↔admin support), and the no-chat/no-forums rule still stands — the platform hosts structured surfaces (jobs board, tutorials, sourcing), never conversation. Earlier plans that leaned on the group (jobs-board-v0-as-group-posts, availability-states-revealed-by-group-chatter, suggestion-portal-as-poll) are void; those signals now have to come from on-platform behaviour and direct member feedback to Nuer.

## Business model & launch state

- **Validation:** A TikTok poll video ("would you pay GHS 100/month?") hit ~4.5K views, 500 likes, 215 comments in ~23h, with dozens of explicit yeses including business accounts. Treated as strong interest signal but NOT payment validation — stated willingness ≠ MoMo payment; planning assumes ~10–15% conversion of commenters.
- **Founding offer (scripted, not yet posted):** 50 founding member spots at **GHS 50/month locked** (vs GHS 100 standard later). Intake: carpenter WhatsApps the word **FOUNDER** to 0244633464 → Nuer collects name/business/area/specialty → adds them to the WhatsApp Founders group AND registers them in the platform via admin dashboard. **Payment is collected only after the group is active** — announced inside the group with notice, manual MoMo, recorded by Nuer in the admin payments UI.
- Registration is **admin-entered by design** in Phase 1 (Nuer vets every founder personally). No public self-serve registration. This is a feature, not a gap.
- Scope decision: **construction/site carpenters are included**, not just furniture makers (specialty vocabulary in the schema reflects this).
- Economics: 50 × GHS 50 = GHS 2,500/mo against ~GHS 150/mo infrastructure. Margin is never the constraint; member acquisition is.
- **Current blocker is NOT code.** The platform is live; the founder video is not recorded. Nothing in the codebase blocks launch. If asked to prioritize, nothing should be built that delays or substitutes for member acquisition.
- **LAUNCHED (owner confirmation, 2026-07-15 evening): the founder video is up and payments are coming in.** The "don't build before launch" constraint has served its purpose; feature work on the member platform is now unblocked, prioritized by the owner's roadmap (jobs board first — see the platform roadmap section below).

## Known technical debts / accepted trade-offs (do not "fix" without instruction)

- **Stateless tokens can't be revoked** — a PIN change doesn't kill an existing token (valid until 30-day expiry). Accepted at 50-vetted-founder scale. Revisit (sessions in D1) only if Concierge ever handles money or messaging directly.
- Payment recording upserts on (member, period) — deliberate, lets Nuer correct mistakes.
- CORS allows bare + www domain. Admin `PUT /members/:phone` can edit profile fields — deliberate, optimizes for owner speed.
- Phone numbers ARE exposed inside the members-only directory — that IS the product (members hire members). Founders are told at intake that joining = being findable.

## Directory upgrade, 15 July 2026 (owner-directed, pre-launch — done deliberately BEFORE founders register)

Owner reviewed the directory ahead of the founder push and directed five changes; all built + deployed same day. The reasoning, for future sessions:

1. **Trade taxonomy revised** (see spec for the list). `furniture` split three ways (cabinet construction / interior work / solid wood furniture); `glass_aluminium` retired; `outdoor_structures` added (owner's "exterior carpentry framework" — pergolas, huts, sheds; "Outdoor structures" was CC's suggested name, owner accepted). **`finishing_spray` was explicitly kept** after CC flagged that removing it (owner's first instinct) would break the canonical "upholsterer hires a spray finisher" use case — owner agreed. `site_construction` kept alongside outdoor_structures: site = structural/formwork/shuttering, outdoor = garden structures. Done at 2 members precisely because a vocabulary change at 50 members would be a real migration; at 2 it's a profile re-edit. **The 2 pre-revision profiles (Nuer, Maxwell) still carry `furniture` — owner should re-save them via admin.** Legacy keys render via a fallback label map and are rejected on save.
2. **Areas are now a fixed zone vocabulary** (`ZONE_GROUPS` in js/concierge.js): Greater Accra zones first, then all 15 other regions as whole-region options — owner: "a large portion of our base is also outside Accra", so regions replaced a single 'Outside Accra' bucket. Free text was fragmenting the area filter before it started. **Maps are deferred by decision** — map tiles are the heaviest thing addable on MTN data; the agreed later path is an optional per-member GPS pin + a "View on map" link (opens the Maps app, zero page weight), only if founders ask.
3. **Compact horizontal cards** (small photo left, details right) — the old big-photo cards wouldn't survive a 50-member list.
4. **Featured rotation + "select your area" directive**: at ≥12 members the directory opens with 6 "Featured today" members (date-seeded shuffle → deterministic, rotates daily, every member featured equally often — fairness is mechanical, nobody can allege favouritism) + a select-your-area prompt + Show-all button; under 12 it shows everyone so a small directory never looks empty. If the owner ever wants to SELL featured slots, that's a new feature to design deliberately — the current rotation is not that.
5. **Photo upload replaced the photo-URL field** ("no one is going to input a URL" — owner). Client-side canvas compression (512px square JPEG, ~30–60KB) BEFORE upload, so a 4MB camera photo never leaves the member's phone; Worker caps at 300KB + checks JPEG magic bytes; one object per member in R2 (`concierge/members/<phone>.jpg`, overwrite-in-place) so storage is bounded at ~3MB for 50 members. The spec addendum's "Cloudflare Images at ~20 photos" trigger was consciously revisited: with client-side compression, R2 alone comfortably carries 50+ members — treat that trigger as "revisit if photos become a burden", not automatic. Trade popovers (tap on mobile — members are on phones, hover doesn't exist there; hover also works on desktop) show what each trade covers, and the same examples appear as hints in the profile-edit trade picker.

## Platform roadmap — "the Uber for carpenters" (owner directive, 2026-07-15 evening)

Owner's post-launch direction, given the same day payments started arriving. The member area will grow into tabs: **Directory · Jobs · Tools · Tutorials · Sourcing**. Decisions on record:

- **Jobs board is the flagship** ("apprentices needed in Prampram" — a business day-hires members already based where the job is, instead of paying hotels for permanent staff; works both directions). Shape agreed in advice: `jobs` table (poster, zone, trade, skill level needed, dates, rate, status open/filled/closed), zone/trade filters, **applications happen via wa.me deep link to the poster — never in-platform messaging**. Not yet built; next major build.
- **Skill levels ✅ BUILT 2026-07-15** (migration 0003: `skill_level` 'apprentice'|'carpenter'|'master' + `years_experience`). **Self-set by members in edit-profile — owner's explicit choice** over CC's admin-set-at-intake recommendation; the mitigation is that admin can correct any member's level inline in the admin table, and CC's warning (self-declared levels inflate) stands on record for when job history can replace badges. Directory cards show a level badge + "· N yrs"; directory has a level filter; admin add-member captures level/years at intake.
- **Member deletion ✅ BUILT 2026-07-15** (owner request — was missing entirely). `DELETE /api/admin/members/:phone` with two client-side sanity checks (type the member's phone, then a final confirm listing what's destroyed) AND two server-side guards (body must echo the phone in `confirm`; admins can never delete themselves — lockout protection). Hard delete: member row, payments (FK forces it — the confirm dialog warns payment history goes too), saved cutlists, login attempts, R2 photo.
- **Tutorials tab (not built):** videos hosted on YouTube/TikTok, never self-hosted (cost + MTN data); the tab is a curated index (title, trade tags, level, link). Ghana-specific content. Content creation is the bottleneck, not code.
- **Sourcing tab (not built; name agreed "Sourcing"):** Ghana-specific knowledge base — entries are either sourcing answers ("lazy susan fittings: where in Accra") or construction workarounds; entries reference a `stores` list (name, zone, WhatsApp). Admin-curated; member tips come to Nuer directly (no submission portal). Partnership ladder agreed: list stores → negotiate member discounts (real leverage: 50 organized carpenters = collective buying power) → partner badge → shared inventory search ONLY if a partner actually has a digital inventory (most don't — design nothing for APIs yet).
- **Open business decisions flagged, not decided:** self-serve registration (schema ready — applicants as `pending`, owner approves; vetting stays the trust engine) and whether job-seekers/apprentices pay the same GHS 50/mo as businesses posting jobs (free-seeker or employer-pays tiers may fit better once jobs exist).

## Phase 2 preview (do not start yet — separate spec will follow)

Safety check-in: `callouts` table (member, client name/phone, location, expected end, status), member-facing create/check-in/check-out flow, **Worker cron trigger** scanning for overdue call-outs every ~15 min, SMS alert to emergency contact + notification to Nuer.
