The Brief

An Entire Centre, In Solid Teak

Sage Experience is a US-based educational NGO founded to build bridges between Ghanaian communities and students and alumni from America's leading universities. Its centre in Wli, in Ghana's Volta Region, was conceived as a residential hub for cultural exchange — a place where Ivy League students and Ghanaian youth could live, learn, and work alongside each other.

The founder, Ben Schwartz — a former Dartmouth College admissions officer with postgraduate degrees from MIT and Harvard — had a clear and uncompromising vision for the space: every piece of furniture, every surface, every fitting would be built from solid Ghanaian teak. Nothing prefabricated. Nothing from a showroom floor. The entire centre, from the hostel rooms to the dining hall to the outdoor terraces, would be furnished by hand.

The scope was unlike anything typically commissioned in Ghana — and the standard expected was international.

The Story

The Only Contractor Who Stayed

Projects like Sage Centre are rare in Ghana — and rarely straightforward. Before Neewoody was brought in, other contractors had taken deposits and left the work incomplete. The client was left exposed, behind schedule, and understandably cautious.

Neewoody arrived and stayed. But more than staying, the process of working on this project forced a genuine raising of standards. Ben Schwartz had an eye for detail that most Ghanaian carpenters — trained in a market that often prioritises speed over precision — were not equipped to meet. Every joint, every surface, every dimension was scrutinised. Problems were identified before they became expensive. Solutions were expected, not excuses.

What could have been a difficult relationship became something more valuable: a 26-month education in building to a different standard. The craftsmanship that left Wli in October 2024 was measurably better than what arrived in June 2022 — and that improvement carries into every Neewoody project that has followed.

The Setting

Wli, Volta Region

Sage Centre sits in Wli — a community in Ghana's Volta Region, nestled at the foot of the Agumatsa Wildlife Sanctuary and the tallest waterfall in West Africa. The site itself is a carefully cultivated property with extensive gardens and unobstructed views of the surrounding mountain range.

The setting informed the brief. A facility built in a place this considered deserved furniture built with the same intention — materials that belonged to the landscape, craftsmanship that would hold up for decades, and a level of finish that matched the ambition of what was being built around it.

Every piece Neewoody built was made from solid Ghanaian teak — a timber that grows in this same region of West Africa and carries its own connection to the land.
