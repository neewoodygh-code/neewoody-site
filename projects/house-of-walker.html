The Brief

A Problem to Solve, Not a Blank Canvas

Johnnie Walker chose XO Lounge at One Airport Square — one of Accra's premium commercial addresses — as the venue for their House of Walker activation, celebrating 200 years of the brand. The space had the right scale and location. But it had a structural problem.

The venue's existing concrete seating — a large curved platform and a central cubby seating area — was too deep for the brand's planned display installations. The geometry didn't work. The displays would sit wrong. The activation couldn't proceed without resolving it.

Neewoody was brought in to fix the problem and deliver a finished installation to Johnnie Walker's exact brand specification: black and gold, with integrated lighting, built fast.

The Challenge

Adapting What Exists to What the Brand Needs

This wasn't a build from scratch. The concrete seating infrastructure was already there — fixed, heavy, and the wrong depth. Neewoody's job was to work with it, not around it.

The central cubby seating units were infilled completely to create flush, usable display surfaces — then finished for tiling by the wider fit-out team. The curved platform was the more visible transformation: structurally modified, then clad in MDF to the brand's black and gold specification, with integrated LED strip lighting channels running the full length of the base.

Every dimension, every colour, every material decision was dictated by Johnnie Walker's brand guidelines. There was no creative latitude — only execution accuracy. Under event pressure, with a fixed deadline and a global brand watching.

What Was Built

Neewoody's Scope

Every item below was designed, modified, built, and finished by Neewoody — to Johnnie Walker's brand specification.
